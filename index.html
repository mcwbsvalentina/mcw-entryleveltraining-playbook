Power Words vs. Doubt Words
Your words shape how customers see you. Replace doubt words with power words to build confidence and close more sales.
✕ Doubt Words (Avoid) ✓ Power Words (Use Instead)
I think…I know…
Maybe…Absolutely…
Hopefully…Definitely…
I'll try…I will…
Probably…For sure…
It should…It will…

Tip: Power words show confidence. Customers trust confident people.
CPR — Creating Personal Relationships
The best salespeople don't just sell, they connect. Use the FORDS method to spark genuine conversation and build rapport before your pitch.
F
Family
Ask about their family, kids, siblings, or loved ones.
"Do you live nearby with your family?"
O
Occupation
Ask about their job or career.
"What do you do for work?"
R
Recreation
Ask about hobbies, interests, or free time activities.
"What do you like to do on weekends?"
D
Dreams
Ask about future goals, trips, or bucket list items.
"If you could travel anywhere, where would you go?"
S
Sports
Ask about favorite teams or sports they follow or play.
"Do you follow football or basketball?"
Tip: Listen actively and reference their answers later in the conversation — it shows you care and makes your pitch more personal.

Theories & Vocabulary
Know these cold. These are the terms, theories, and frameworks your whole team runs on.
Holster Theory
Keep your entry level near you at all times so you can make sure they are learning and developing from the start. Introduce them to the people that are the best examples of the business.
Build, Coach, Build (BCB)
A method of training. Never teach someone by destroying or breaking their confidence. Always start by building them up, then point out what they need to work on, and then follow up with positive reinforcement to build their confidence again.
Feel, Felt, Found
A method used to empathize vs sympathize. Use it in training or in the field.
"I know exactly how you feel, a lot of people felt the same way, but what I have found is..."
KISS
Keep It Short and Simple. Condense large bodies of information to the main points your entry level or customer needs to know.
BEAST Factors
Body language, Eye contact, Awesome Attitude, Smile, Timing and Tonality. This is what separates you from a telemarketer and gives you the ability to have a real relationship with a person. Your first impression needs to be great. Smiling makes people want to talk to you. Eye contact conveys honesty. Your attitude will get your customer excited — if you are not excited, they will not be excited.
CPR
Create Personal Relationships. Use FORDS to build genuine rapport before your pitch.
DM — Decision Maker
You are looking for a person that can place an order TODAY without asking permission.
Buying Sign
A sign that your customer is ready for the close. Things to look for: nodding, asking questions, mirroring, removing distractions, yes train, offering services, complaining, and silence — this is the most commonly missed buying sign.
Closing Statement
Moving from the presentation into an actual sale. Close at the height of impulse and assume the sale. At this point you have to take control and get their info into the iPad.
Bullets
Benefits of whatever you are offering — retail gift cards, free installation, waived activation, no down payment, priority shipping. Only give one bullet at a time and then attempt to close after each one.
FORDS
Family, Occupation, Recreation, Dreams & Dogs, School & Sports. All ways to build rapport with people and take the pressure off of the sale.
5 Steps to a Conversation
Every single conversation follows these 5 steps. Know the purpose of each one and why we do it.
1
Introduction
Purpose: Remove skepticism, break the ice
How: BEAST factors
Why: Sales are a transfer of energy. The product sells itself — you've got to get people to buy into YOU
2
Short Story
Purpose: Qualify the customer
How: Ask qualifying questions
Why: Our time is valuable. We want to spend it with decision-makers, not time wasters
3
Presentation
Purpose: Build impulse on the hot button — sell the sizzle
How: FUJI factors
Why: We sell a quality product. We want people to fall in love with the product AND the price. We do impulse sales — we want the customer to act NOW. As soon as they walk away, chances of closing are slim to none
4
Close
Purpose: Take control
How: Set expectations and rebuild impulse
Why: We are collecting people's personal information — keep them in the loop every step of the way. We run info with the intention to close, get installed, and get paid. Also: integrity
5
Rehash
Purpose: Maximize profits
How: Set expectations
Why: We close sales with the intention for them to get activated/installed and get paid. Make sure all bases are covered — install date, canceling current provider, receiving gift card, returning trade-ins
4’s — FUJI Factors (4 Factors of Impulse)
Use these to build impulse during your presentation. Don’t forget to reclose and ask for the zip code!
F
Fear of Loss
Putting QUANTITY on their hot button — make them feel like they could miss out
U
Urgency
Putting a TIME on a hot button — create a deadline that makes them act now
J
Jones Effect
Using a Customer vs Customer approach — everyone else is doing it, they don’t want to be left behind
I
Indifference
The opposite of a salesperson. Don’t be desperate — be relaxed, confident, and let them come to you

Dangling the Carrot
Making the customer feel like they can’t get something — reeling them in slow and steady
Power / Pregnant Pauses
Pregnant: Inserting a dramatic pause before delivering some really great news
Power: Inserting a dramatic pause after delivering some really great news
Reverse Close
Ultimately switching the script. Role-playing that the customer already has the great deal you’re offering them and you are now a salesperson offering them what they already have.
This works especially well when highlighting their hot buttons.
8 Great Work Habits
1
Always maintain a great positive attitudeHaving a great attitude is nothing if you’re not able to maintain it throughout the day
2
Be on timeBeing on time takes no talent or skill. It shows you’re dedicated. When you’re late, you miss out on important information
3
Be preparedClothes ready the night before, two pens, iPad charged, gas in the car, know where your keys are — just be a responsible adult
4
Work a full dayPhysically being there and mentally being there are two different things. Do both. You’re going to be there till 7pm anyway — might as well make money
5
Work your territory correctlyDon’t pitch in areas where you’re not supposed to. Keep your head on a swivel and greet people when they enter your vicinity
6
Respect clients and customersRespect everyone. We won’t make money if we don’t have stores. The most important customers are the ones who tell us no
7
Understand your opportunityWe aren’t here just to sell AT&T. It’s much bigger than that. Don’t ruin it for yourself and definitely don’t ruin it for others
8
Take controlOf your pitch, your attitude, your time, your life. You’re in charge — act like it
Law of Averages (LOA)
Sales is a numbers game. The more you do something, the more chances you have to succeed at it.
1
The more you do something the more chances you have to succeed at it
2
If you can maintain your attitude and give the same pitch to as many customers as possible, you will succeed
3
Knowing your numbers and what you need to focus on to get better will increase your results and make you a great sales trainer
